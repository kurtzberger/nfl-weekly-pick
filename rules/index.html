Scoring

The scoring for this league will follow the "Straight-Up Weekly Ranked Pick 'em" format. That is, each week, along with picking each team you predict to win, you will also rank each game 1 to the number of games played that week. For example, if there are 16 games to be played for that week, you will rank those games 1 through 16. Please note that "Straight-Up" means the spread is NOT a factor in determining if your pick was correct or not. You only have to worry about picking the winner of each game. Note: if a game ends in a tie, the corresponding pick is marked incorrect.

Upon completion of a game*, if your prediction was correct, you will receive points equal to the rank that you assigned for the corresponding game; however, if your prediction was incorrect, you will lose those points. Because of this, it is recommended to rank games you are more confident with a higher point value and rank games you are either unsure of or attempting to pick an upset with a lower point value. Please note: your season starting score will be 0, but there is no minimum score, meaning it is possible for your score to drop below zero.

There are no weekly winners. The winner of the league is determined by the player with the most points at the end of the NFL regular season (week 17).

Tiebreaker

In the event that two or more players have the same amount of points, the tiebreaker will go to the player who has the higher win percentage. Win percentage is calculated by the player's correctly predicted games divided by the number of completed games.

$$ winPercent = {numberOfWins \over numberOfCompleteGames} $$

Missed Picks & Unassigned Points

Any pick(s) that were not made prior to game time will be marked as incorrect at completion of that game. The corresponding unassigned point(s) will be subtracted from your point total.

For example: In a week with 15 games played, two picks were left unmade. The two unassigned points were 5 and 1 for a total of 6 unassigned points. After calculating the score (using the rules stated above), those 6 points will be subtracted from the total.

*NFL game scores are pulled from a free API by NFL.com. Final scores may be delayed several minutes.

Making Your Picks

To make your picks, click the menu button in the upper left corner, select "Your Picks", and then select the desired week. The current NFL week will be marked with bold text.

After navigating to the desired week, a table will be loaded containing all NFL games of that week (see screenshot below). Any picks that you have already submitted will be loaded into the table as well.

Use the radio buttons to select either the home or away team, and then use the drop down menu under the "Assigned Points" column to assign a point value to your pick.

Clearing a Pick

If you wish clear a pick, use the corresponding reset button on the right of each row. Not all the picks must be submitted together, so the resetting feature is useful when you have mistakenly made a pick before wanting submit it. If your intention is to clear a pick that you have already submitted before, be sure to submit your picks before navigating away from the page to ensure this change is reflected in the database.

Submitting Your Picks

Use the submit button at the bottom of the page to submit your picks. An error message will be displayed if attempting to submit an incomplete pick. An incomplete pick can be either: A team was selected but no point value assigned, or a point value was assigned but no team selected. To correct this error, you must either clear the pick (no errors occur with cleared picks), or complete it by selecting a team or assigning a point value (depending on which is missing).

Pick Deadlines

Picks may be changed and resubmitted as often as you'd like; however, picks will be locked within 10 seconds after kickoff. The kickoff date and time for each game is listed under the "Date" column.

Viewing Picks

To view picks made by the all players, click the menu button in the upper left corner, select "League Picks", and then select the desired week. The current NFL week will be marked with bold text.

After navigating to the desired week, two tables will be loaded (see screenshot below). The table displayed on top will have the current week's NFL matchups with kickoff time, visitor & home scores, and the current quarter.

Here all picks, other than your own, will be hidden until kickoff. This is to prevent other players from seeing each other's picks before the picks locked.
